World Capitals

A capital city is extremely important to a country since it is where all the governmental functions take place. The legislature, parliament, and other governmental buildings are built in the capital, and therefore the big leaders and officials of the country live in this city.



Canada

Ottawa is located in Ontario, Canada in the southeastern area, bordering Quebec. It's population is recorded as 951,727 in 2014, making it the fourth largest city in the country. The city was founded in 1826 as "Bytown", and governed as "Ottawa" in 1855. Ottawa is home to the Parliament of Canada, Supreme Court of Canada, Bank of Canada building, and many other government buildings. Ottawa has the highest standard of living in the nation and low unemployment rates, making it 2nd place out of 150 worldwide in the quality of life index.

Iran

Tehran is located in Tehran province in Iran and is considered the 2nd largest city in Western Asia, with a population of over 9 million, and 16 million in the wider metropolitan area, making it the 3rd most populated in the Middle East, and 29th in the world (metropolitan area population). It was first chosen as the capital in 1796, and even after the capital was changed multiple times over the course of history, it is known as the national capital of the country.

Some of the most popular architectures in the city include the Azadi Tower, and Milad Tower (as shown in the picture) which is known as the world's 17th tallest structure. Due to extreme air pollution in Tehran, there have been rumors about plans to relocate Iran's capital to a different city.

Germany

Berlin is the 2nd most populous city proper and 7th most populous urban are in the European Union with 3.5 million citizens. Due to the location of berlin in the northeast of Germany, it benefits from a rather tropical climate, and therefore, a third of the city is made up of forests, parks, gardens, rivers, and lakes. After the WW2, the city was divided into two parts, East Berlin which became the capital of East Germany, and West Berlin, with the Berlin Wall seperating the two. In 1990, the Berlin Wall was broken down and the two parts reunited during the German Reunification. Berlin has incredibly successful technological and research facilities, media corporations and convention venues. The city also has a high number of cultural venues which include festivals, galleries and museums, opera houses, and many others.
